Synergizing analytical instrumentation and multivariate statistics

Many of the world's untapped resources are too heterogeneous to see industrial use. Lignin is a prime example: it is a complex heteropolymer component of plant-based biomass that produces thousands of chemical moieties when deconstructed. Understanding these lignin breakdown product distributions requires a combination of analytical and mathematical tools. Within this domain, I specialize in factor analysis of spectral data.

Ponukumati, A.; Gao, Y.; Li, H.; Walker, M.; Zou, X.; Jeon, S.; Barrett, J.; Hosseinaei, O.; Harper, D.; Ford, P.; Williams, B.; Foston, M.; Chemical Profiling Catalytic Lignin Depolymerization Using Positive Matrix Factorization Deconvolution of Mass Spectral Data. Submitted: RSC Green Chemistry. (2023).

Modeling the time evolution of chemical catalytic systems

Chemical kinetics is the study of time, and I want to see into the future. Seeing into the future is especially necessary for heterogeneous catalytic systems, which are seldom as simple as A → B. My work focuses on monitoring chemical processes over time and creating models that simulate their evolution. This can take the form of routine rate constant calculations, suggesting reaction pathways and networks, global fitting, or building population balance equations from first principles.

Zhang, J.; Yang, Z.; Ponukumati, A.; Senanayake, M.; Pingali, S. V.; Foston, M.; Structural Evolution of Lignin Using in-situ Small Angle Neutron Scattering During Catalytic Disassembly. Submitted: RSC Green Chemistry. (2023).

Integrating concepts from biological and chemical engineering

Much of the world's most complex chemistry is conveniently packaged in the form of living organisms. We can use these microorganisms and their metabolic pathways to produce speciality commodities from chemical cocktails. Because traditional chemical engineering techniques cannot handle this alone, I design systems to produce such value-added products using chemical and biological means in a hybrid manner.

Ponukumati, A.; Carr, R.; Gao, Y.; Shang, Z.; Krishnamurthy, A.; Moon, T.-S.; Foston, M.; Integrating lignin-first biorefining and biological funneling using wild-type Rhodococcus opacus PD630. Manuscript in progress.

Applying trends in human decision-making to online cheat detection

The integrity of any society relies on trust, and chess is no exception. Unfortunately, mechanisms behind fair play enforcement systems have been kept under lock and key for the better part of 30 years. This has seriously battered down trust and stunted research in the field. As a result, current anti-cheat mechanisms are simply not capable of catching the most elusive cheaters and are very prone to false positive detections. I'm intent on open collaboration in this area, and I champion player behavior analysis as the proper way to develop an online chess cheat detection framework.

Laarhoven, T.; Ponukumati, A.; Towards Transparent Cheat Detection in Online Chess: An Application of Human and Computer Decision-Making Preferences. Computers & Games. (2023). https://doi.org/10.1007/978-3-031-34017-8_14.

Finding opportunities in the global small value stock universe

Small stocks, on average, have higher expected returns than large stocks. This is largely, but not entirely, due to small stocks being riskier than large stocks. Lack of coverage and unfamiliarity biases are partly to blame, but ultimately companies are priced on fundamental bases. Taking risks is essential — I believe that investing in burgeoning small companies, with relatively poor fundamentals but promising ideas, has a much larger social footprint than investing in any other securities. Such firms tend to be in the energy and materials sectors, including many chemical firms.
